Ongoing Project Details

Smart Solar Soil Monitoring System with Real-Time Mobile Alerts

Role: Team Leader
Status: Implementation Phase
Power System: Photovoltaic (Solar) Array

Project Overview

Agriculture suffers from over-irrigation or water depletion due to unmonitored soil. This project creates an autonomous system leveraging Internet of Things (IoT) sensors and micro-controllers (e.g. ESP32/Arduino) run on solar batteries. Sensors measure moisture, chemical pH, and temperature. An integrated AI-model runs predictions on whether plants require water based on weather forecast trends, sending alerts to farmer mobile devices via GSM or Wi-Fi notifications.

Key Architectural Features

  • Energy Independence: 10W solar panels charging a lithium battery pack for continuous field operations.
  • Sensor Arrays: Soil hygrometers, NPK chemical sensors, and digital DHT thermometers.
  • Edge Processing: Intelligent forecasting rules on the micro-controller to determine critical trigger thresholds.
  • Alert System: Custom notifications push to mobile displays immediately upon threshold breaches.

Leadership Responsibilities (Sunera Mehak)

  • Allocating electrical wiring and code-construction modules to team members.
  • Coordinating circuit design assemblies with firmware coders.
  • Setting project schedules and tracking milestones to verify execution deliverables.
